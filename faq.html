Frequently Asked Questions


How many lessons will I need?

Short answer, it depends on the individual. Some people learn to ride quickly while others need some more time to fully grasp the concepts. But in general, for someone who knows nothing about motorcycles, it takes about 3-4 lessons to firmly understand both the fundamental concepts and safety techniques of riding a motorcycle.








What do I need to bring?

Nothing. The motorcycle and helmet are provided for you. You should however wear a long sleeve shirt, long pants, and solid closed toe shoes.




What motorcycle do I get to learn on?

In San Diego, a Kawasaki Ninja 250. In New York, a Honda CBR 250.
250cc motorcycles are much lighter and less powerful than other
motorcycles hence making them ideal candidates for learning
to ride a motorcycle.


















                                               What do I need to bring?

                                                                            Nothing. The motorcycle and helmet are provided for you. You should however wear a long sleeve shirt, long pants, and solid closed toe shoes.











What will I learn?

Everything you need to know to ride safely on the streets will be covered. This includes the fundamental concepts (basic controls, throttle acceleration, braking, turning, shifting gears,etc.) and safety techniques (emergency braking, uphill starting/stopping, tight U turns, countersteering, etc.)


























What do I do after the lessons?

Get your riding gear, a motorcycle permit, and insurance. Then go buy a motorcycle. Start riding, and once you feel you're ready, set up a DMV appointment to take the motorcycle skills test for your M1 license. You're always welcome to text/call us for more advice as you go along your process of getting started with motorcycles. Most of all, good luck and stay safe out there.
