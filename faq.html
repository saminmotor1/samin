Frequently Asked Questions


How many lessons will I need?

Short answer, it depends on the individual. Some people learn to ride quickly while others need some more time to fully grasp the concepts. But in general, for someone who knows nothing about motorcycles, it takes about 3-4 lessons to firmly understand both the fundamental concepts and safety techniques of riding a motorcycle.



We can't stress enough the importance of properly learning to ride a motorcycle and not rushing through the process. Your life and health is at risk so this isn't something you should take lightly and try to 'save' as much money as possible on. Which would you rather do: Spend a couple more bucks on lessons learning to ride a motorcycle OR thousands on hospital and medical bills from an accident?

Unfortunately, over the years we have seen many people go through pain and suffering from a completely avoidable accident had the individual achieved a great understanding of controlling a motorcycle. Obviously, we have a financial incentive for you to learn with us. But most of all, we want you to avoid this type of tragedy. We want you to be safe.



What's the best first motorcycle?

This is very dependent upon the person. A 4'10 soccer mom would probably want a very different motorcycle than a 250 lb, 6'2 Samoan who is built for the WWE. Visit our New Rider's Video Guide where we discuss this topic and much more regarding getting started with motorcycles. You're more than welcome ask us any questions specific to your situation via messaging on Facebook or Instagram and we'll get back to you as soon as possible.








What motorcycle do I get to learn on?

In San Diego, a Kawasaki Ninja 250. In New York, a Honda CBR 250.
250cc motorcycles are much lighter and less powerful than other
motorcycles hence making them ideal candidates for learning
to ride a motorcycle.





What do I need to bring?

Nothing. The motorcycle and helmet are provided for you. You should however wear a long sleeve shirt, long pants, and solid closed toe shoes.






What will I learn?

Everything you need to know to ride safely on the streets will be covered. This includes the fundamental concepts (basic controls, throttle acceleration, braking, turning, shifting gears,etc.) and safety techniques (emergency braking, uphill starting/stopping, tight U turns, countersteering, etc.)




What do I do after the lessons?

Get your riding gear and a motorcycle permit. Then buy a motorcycle and insurance. Start riding, and once you've got some experience under your belt and you feel you're ready (typically 2-3 months riding experience minimum), set up a DMV appointment to take the motorcycle skills test for your M1 license. You're always welcome to ask us for more advice as you go along your process of getting started with motorcycles. The easiest way to reach us is to send a message through Facebook or Instagram. Most of all, good luck and stay safe out there.
